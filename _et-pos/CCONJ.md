Definition


A coordinating conjunction is a word that links words or larger constituents without syntactically subordinating one to the other and expresses a semantic relationship between them.<br/>
The following words are annotated as coordinated conjunctions <code>CONJ</code> in Estonian UD: <i>aga</i> "but", <i>ega</i> "nor", <i>ehk</i> "or", <i>elik</i> "or", <i>ent</i> "but", <i>ja</i> "and", <i>kui</i> "if, when", <i>kuid</i> "but", <i>kuni</i> "until", <i>ning</i> "and", <i>vaid</i> "but" <i>v√µi</i> "or".
For subordinating conjunctions, see [u-dep/sconj]().
