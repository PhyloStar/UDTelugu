In Naija, `Case` is an inflectional feature of [pronouns](pcm-pos/PRON).

Case helps specify the role of the noun phrase in the sentence. 

### <a name="Nom">`Nom`</a>: nominative

Typically used when a personal pronoun is used as the subject of a clause.

#### Examples

* _<b>a</b>_ "I"
* _<b>you</b>_ "you"
* _<b>im</b>_ 
* _<b>e</b>_ "it"
* _<b>we</b>_ "we"
* _<b>una</b>_ "you guys"
* _<b>dem</b>_ "them"
* _<b>de</b>_ "they"


### <a name="Acc">`Acc`</a>: accusative

Typically used when a personal pronoun is used as a complement for a predicate.

#### Examples

* _<b>me</b>_ "me"
* _<b>you</b>_ "you"
* _<b>am</b>_ "him"
* _<b>us</b>_ "us"
* _<b>una</b>_ "you guys"
* _<b>dem</b>_ "them"
